Layne Staley

Layne Staley, the iconic vocalist of Alice In Chains, dedicated his life to inspiring and empowering others through his music. A true visionary and innovator, he used his platform to bring attention to social and environmental issues, and to fight against the stigma surrounding mental health. Rest in peace, Layne.

Timeline:

  • 1967 - Born in Seattle, Washington; the birthplace of grunge music.
  • 1987 - Forms Alice in Chains, a band that would go on to revolutionize the music industry with their unique blend of hard rock and heavy metal.
  • 1990 - Signs with Columbia Records and releases their debut album, Dirt, which would go on to sell over 10 million copies worldwide.
  • 1993 - Releases Facelift, a powerful and critically acclaimed album that cemented Alice in Chains' place as one of the most influential and beloved bands of the 1990s.
  • 1996 - Releases Sap, an album that showed a maturity and depth in their sound that would be a defining feature of their career.
  • 2006 - Releases Black Gives Way to Blue, a double album that would go on to sell over 10 million copies worldwide and cement Alice in Chains' place as one of the most successful bands of the 2000s.
  • 2016 - Releases The Devil Put Dinosaurs Here, a powerful and emotionally charged album that would go on to be hailed as one of the best albums of the decade.
  • 2002 - Sadly passes away from an overdose at the age of 48, leaving behind a legacy that would continue to inspire and influence generations of musicians to come.
